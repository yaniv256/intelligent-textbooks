## About This MicroSim

This MicroSim visualizes David Wiley's **5Rs framework** for Open Educational Resources (OER) — Retain, Reuse, Revise, Remix, and Redistribute — as a vertical Mermaid.js flowchart, starting from a licensed OER and building cumulatively toward full redistribution rights. A seventh node, styled with a dashed purple border, presents a proposed **6th R: Regenerate** — the freedom to recreate an intelligent textbook from its underlying learning graph and reusable agentic skills, not just from its rendered chapters.

See the full discussion in the [5Rs of Intelligent Textbooks appendix](../../appendices/five-rs-of-intelligent-textbooks.md).

## How to Use

- **Hover** over any step in the diagram to see a short, one-sentence tip in a floating tooltip.
- **Click** a step to select it (highlighted with an amber border) and read a detailed narrative explanation in the right-hand panel.
- The color key at the top of the right panel distinguishes the licensed OER starting point, Wiley's original 5Rs, and the proposed 6th R.

## Lesson Plan

### Grade Level
Undergraduate, graduate, and professional development (instructional designers, OER authors, textbook publishers)

### Duration
10-15 minutes

### Prerequisites
General familiarity with copyright vs. open licensing (e.g. Creative Commons) is helpful but not required.

### Activities

1. **Exploration** (5 min): Click through each of the 5Rs in order and read the detailed narrative for each, noting the example given for each freedom.
2. **Guided Practice** (5 min): Discuss why each R is described as building cumulatively on the one before it (e.g. why you can't Remix a resource you have no right to Revise).
3. **Assessment** (5 min): Click Regenerate and discuss whether it should count as a true 6th R or as an implementation detail of the existing 5Rs.

### Assessment
Ask learners to classify a real resource (e.g. a textbook PDF found online) against the 5Rs: which freedoms does its license actually grant?
